The 3 Ways Data Centers Make Money (And What Each Means for Your Returns)

Global Data Center Hub • Global Data Center Hub • Oct 17
Score: 0.554
1,605 words
2 comments
11 likes

Data centers generate revenue through three distinct models that cater to different customer needs and offer varying risk-return profiles for investors. The choice of revenue model significantly impacts pricing, cash flow stability, operational complexity, and long-term returns, with successful platforms often blending all three approaches.

•
Retail Colocation: Customers rent individual racks or small spaces with high margins but greater operational complexity, requiring intensive customer service and frequent contract renewals
•
Wholesale Colocation: Clients lease entire suites (1-20 MW) on 5-15 year terms with lower unit margins but predictable cash flow and reduced churn
•
Hyperscale Leasing: Long-term partnerships with tech giants like Microsoft and Google involving massive facilities (100+ MW) with compressed margins but exceptional stability and scale
•
AI-Driven Evolution: GPU colocation and compute-as-a-service models are emerging, with operators like CoreWeave blending physical infrastructure with cloud economics to capture both infrastructure and workload margins
